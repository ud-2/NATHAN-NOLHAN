### SECTION 1: Number and Calculation (Unit 1) - [18 Marks]

**Question 1: Surds and Irrational Numbers [6 marks]**
a) Explain, using mathematical reasoning, why $\sqrt{90}$ is an irrational number, but $\sqrt{2.25}$ is a rational number. **[2]**
b) Without using a calculator, expand and simplify fully: 
   $$ (3\sqrt{5} - 2)(\sqrt{5} + 4) $$ **[3]**
c) Between which two consecutive integers does the value of $\sqrt[3]{200}$ lie? **[1]**

**Question 2: Advanced Indices [6 marks]**
a) Simplify the following expression completely, leaving your answer as a single power of $a$:
   $$ \frac{(a^3)^4 \times a^{-5}}{a^2 \times a^{-1}} $$ **[3]**
b) Solve for $x$ in the following equation:
   $$ 3^{2x - 1} = \frac{1}{81} $$ **[3]**

**Question 3: Standard Form in Context [6 marks]**
The speed of light is approximately $3 \times 10^8$ meters per second. 
The distance from the Earth to a distant star is $4.5 \times 10^{13}$ kilometers.
a) Convert the distance to the star into **meters**, giving your answer in standard form. **[2]**
b) Calculate how many **seconds** it takes for light from the star to reach Earth. Give your answer in standard form. **[2]**
c) Convert your answer from part (b) into **years** (assume 1 year = 365 days). Round your answer to the nearest whole year. **[2]**

### SECTION 2: Expressions and Formulae (Unit 2) - [22 Marks]

**Question 4: Constructing and Expanding Expressions [7 marks]**
A rectangular swimming pool has a length of $(3x + 5)$ meters and a width of $(2x - 3)$ meters. A paved walkway of width $2$ meters is built entirely around the pool.
a) Write down an expression, in terms of $x$, for the **total length** and the **total width** of the pool including the walkway. **[2]**
b) Expand and simplify an expression for the **total area** of the pool AND the walkway combined. **[3]**
c) If $x = 4$, calculate the actual area of the paved walkway alone. **[2]**

**Question 5: Deriving and Changing the Subject [9 marks]**
In physics, the period $T$ of a pendulum is given by the formula:
$$ T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}} $$
where $L$ is the length and $g$ is gravity.
a) Calculate the value of $T$ when $L = 2.5$, $g = 9.8$, and $\pi \approx 3.142$. Give your answer to 2 decimal places. **[2]**
b) Rearrange the formula to make $L$ the subject. Show every step of your algebraic manipulation. **[4]**
c) Another kinematic formula is $v^2 = u^2 + 2as$. Make $u$ the subject of this formula. **[3]**

**Question 6: Algebraic Fractions [6 marks]**
Simplify the following algebraic expression fully by writing it as a single fraction:
$$ \frac{3x - 1}{4} - \frac{x + 2}{3} $$ **[6]**

### SECTION 3: Decimals, Percentages and Rounding (Unit 3) - [18 Marks]

**Question 7: Compound Percentages and Comparison [8 marks]**
Isabella wants to invest $\$15,000$ for $4$ years. She has two options:
*   **Bank A:** Offers $4.5\%$ compound interest per year.
*   **Bank B:** Offers $6\%$ compound interest for the first year, and then $3.5\%$ compound interest for the remaining years.

a) Calculate the total value of the investment in **Bank A** after 4 years. **[2]**
b) Calculate the total value of the investment in **Bank B** after 4 years. **[4]**
c) Which bank should Isabella choose, and by how much money is that option better? **[2]**

**Question 8: Upper and Lower Bounds [10 marks]**
A metal block has a mass $M = 450 \text{ g}$ (measured correct to the nearest $10 \text{ g}$) and a volume $V = 62 \text{ cm}^3$ (measured correct to the nearest integer). 
Density is calculated using the formula: $\text{Density} = \frac{\text{Mass}}{\text{Volume}}$
a) Write down the upper bound and lower bound for the Mass ($M$). **[2]**
b) Write down the upper bound and lower bound for the Volume ($V$). **[2]**
c) Calculate the **upper bound** for the Density of the metal. Give your answer to 2 decimal places. *(Hint: Think carefully about how to maximize a fraction).* **[3]**
d) Calculate the **lower bound** for the Density of the metal. Give your answer to 2 decimal places. **[3]**

### SECTION 4: Equations and Inequalities (Unit 4) - [22 Marks]

**Question 9: Simultaneous Equations in Real Life [8 marks]**
A museum charges different entry fees for adults and children.
A group of $4$ adults and $3$ children pays a total of $\$85$.
A group of $5$ adults and $2$ children pays a total of $\$94$.
Let $a$ represent the cost of an adult ticket and $c$ represent the cost of a child ticket.
a) Construct a pair of simultaneous equations from this information. **[2]**
b) Solve the simultaneous equations algebraically to find the exact cost of one adult ticket and one child ticket. **[5]**
c) How much would a group of $2$ adults and $5$ children pay? **[1]**

**Question 10: Advanced Linear Equations [6 marks]**
Solve the following equation. Show all steps and verify your answer.
$$ 3(2x - 5) + 4 = 5(x + 1) - 2x $$ **[6]**

**Question 11: Deep Inequalities [8 marks]**
A triangle has side lengths of $x \text{ cm}$, $(x + 4) \text{ cm}$, and $15 \text{ cm}$.
The perimeter of the triangle must be **strictly greater than** $31 \text{ cm}$ but **less than or equal to** $45 \text{ cm}$.
a) Construct a double inequality representing the perimeter of the triangle. **[2]**
b) Solve the inequality to find the range of possible values for $x$. **[3]**
c) If $x$ must be an **integer**, list all the possible values for $x$. **[1]**
d) From your list in part (c), explain why $x$ cannot be $5$ due to the fundamental properties of a triangle. *(Hint: Triangle Inequality Theorem)*. **[2]**
