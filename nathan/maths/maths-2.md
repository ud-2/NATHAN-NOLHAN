### SECTION 1: Unit 10 - Graphs

**Question 1: Linear Functions**
The equation of a line is given by $y = 3x - 2$.
*Simple:*
a) Complete the coordinate for this line: $(4, \dots)$.
b) Complete the coordinate for this line: $(\dots, -8)$.
c) What is the gradient of this line?
*Difficult:*
d) Does the point $(15, 43)$ lie on this line? Show algebraic proof.
e) Write down the equation of a line that is **parallel** to this line and passes through the point $(0, 7)$.
f) Algebraically find the exact coordinates of the intersection point between $y = 3x - 2$ and the line $y = -2x + 13$.

**Question 2: Gradient and Intercept**
Consider the linear equation: $2y + 8x = 10$.
*Simple:*
a) Rearrange the equation into the form $y = mx + c$.
b) State the $y$-intercept of this line.
c) State the gradient of this line.
*Difficult:*
d) Calculate the gradient of a new line that passes through the points $(-2, 5)$ and $(4, 17)$.
e) Find the full equation of the line passing through the points in part (d).
f) A third line has a gradient of $\frac{1}{2}$ and passes through $(6, k)$. If its $y$-intercept is $-4$, find the value of $k$.

**Question 3: Interpreting Real-Life Graphs (Distance-Time)**
A car travels from Town A to Town B. It drives $60 \text{ km}$ in $1 \text{ hour}$, rests for $0.5 \text{ hours}$, and then drives the remaining $40 \text{ km}$ in $0.5 \text{ hours}$.
*Simple:*
a) What is the total distance between Town A and Town B?
b) What is the total time taken for the whole journey?
c) What was the car's speed during the first hour?
*Difficult:*
d) Calculate the **average speed** for the entire journey, including the rest stop.
e) Write down the linear equation (in the form $d = mt + c$) that represents the final $0.5$ hours of the journey.
f) A truck leaves Town A exactly $1 \text{ hour}$ after the car. It travels at a constant speed of $50 \text{ km/h}$ without stopping. At what time (in hours from the car's departure) will the truck pass the resting car?

**Question 4: Non-Linear Graphs (Quadratics)**
Consider the quadratic function $y = x^2 - 9$.
*Simple:*
a) Find the value of $y$ when $x = 0$.
b) Find the value of $y$ when $x = 4$.
c) Since $x^2$ is always positive or zero, what is the minimum value of $y$?
*Difficult:*
d) Solve the equation $x^2 - 9 = 0$ to find the coordinates where the curve crosses the $x$-axis.
e) Using algebra, find the exact values of $x$ when $y = 7$.
f) Find the equation of the straight line that connects the two points on the curve where $y = 16$.

### SECTION 2: Unit 11 - Ratio and Proportion

**Question 5: Ratios and Sharing**
*Simple:*
a) Simplify the ratio $45 : 75$ fully.
b) Share $\$200$ in the ratio $3 : 5$.
c) In a ratio of $4 : 7$, the smaller share is $24 \text{ kg}$. What is the larger share?
*Difficult:*
d) Alex, Ben, and Chloe share money in the ratio $2 : 3 : x$. Ben gets $\$60$ and the total amount shared is $\$220$. Find the value of $x$.
e) The ratio of boys to girls in a club is $4 : 5$. When $6$ more boys join, the ratio becomes $1 : 1$. How many girls are in the club?
f) The ratio of the **areas** of two similar triangles is $16 : 49$. What is the ratio of their perimeters?

**Question 6: Direct Proportion**
*Simple:*
a) $y$ is directly proportional to $x$. When $x = 3$, $y = 15$. Find the constant of proportionality, $k$.
b) Write the formula linking $y$ and $x$.
c) Find $y$ when $x = 8$.
*Difficult:*
d) $P$ is directly proportional to the **square** of $Q$ ($P \propto Q^2$). When $Q = 4$, $P = 48$. Find the formula linking $P$ and $Q$.
e) Using your formula from (d), find the exact positive value of $Q$ when $P = 300$.
f) If the value of $Q$ is increased by $10\%$, by what exact percentage does $P$ increase?

**Question 7: Inverse Proportion**
*Simple:*
a) 4 decorators take 6 days to paint a house. How many days would it take 1 decorator working at the same rate?
b) How many days would it take 3 decorators?
c) Is "the speed of a train and the time it takes to travel $100 \text{ km}$" an example of direct or inverse proportion?
*Difficult:*
d) $W$ is inversely proportional to $R$. When $R = 5$, $W = 12$. Find the formula linking $W$ and $R$.
e) Find the value of $R$ when $W = 2.5$.
f) Prove algebraically that if $R$ is doubled, the value of $W$ is exactly halved.

**Question 8: Rates and Scales**
*Simple:*
a) A map has a scale of $1 : 50,000$. A road is $4 \text{ cm}$ on the map. What is its real length in cm?
b) Convert your answer from (a) into kilometers.
c) An exchange rate is $\$1 = €0.85$. Convert $\$60$ into Euros.
*Difficult:*
d) A field has an area of $5 \text{ cm}^2$ on a map with a scale of $1 : 20,000$. Calculate the real area of the field in square kilometers ($\text{km}^2$).
e) $A$ is directly proportional to $B$, and $B$ is inversely proportional to $C$. When $C = 2$, $B = 10$. When $B = 5$, $A = 15$. Formulate a single equation linking $A$ directly to $C$.
f) Two linked gears have $20$ teeth and $50$ teeth. If the smaller gear turns $75$ revolutions per minute, how many revolutions per minute does the larger gear make?

### SECTION 3: Unit 12 - Probability

**Question 9: Basic and Mutually Exclusive Events**
A fair, 6-sided die is rolled.
*Simple:*
a) What is the probability of rolling a $5$?
b) What is the probability of rolling a prime number?
c) The probability of an event happening is $0.28$. What is the probability of it NOT happening?
*Difficult:*
d) A biased die has a probability of $0.3$ for rolling a $6$. The other five numbers have an equal probability of appearing. Calculate the probability of rolling a $2$.
e) Are the events "rolling an even number" and "rolling a multiple of 3" mutually exclusive? Explain mathematically.
f) A factory produces lightbulbs. The probability of a defective bulb is $x$. Out of $500$ bulbs, the expected number of defective bulbs is $15$. Find $x$ as a fraction.

**Question 10: Independent Events**
A spinner has 4 equal sections numbered 1, 2, 3, 4. A coin is flipped.
*Simple:*
a) List all possible outcomes of spinning the spinner and flipping the coin (e.g., 1H, 1T...).
b) How many total outcomes are there in the sample space?
c) What is the probability of getting a "3" and a "Heads"?
*Difficult:*
d) A coin is flipped 4 times in a row. What is the total number of possible outcomes?
e) Calculate the exact probability of getting exactly 3 Heads in those 4 coin flips.
f) A game costs $\$3$ to play. You flip a coin and roll a fair die. If you get "Heads AND a 6", you win $\$24$. Otherwise, you win nothing. Calculate the expected profit or loss per game.

**Question 11: Combined Events (Without Replacement)**
A bag contains 5 Red discs and 3 Blue discs.
*Simple:*
a) If you pick one disc, what is the probability it is Red?
b) You pick a Red disc and do NOT put it back. How many discs are left in the bag in total?
c) Following (b), what is the probability that the second disc picked is also Red?
*Difficult:*
d) Draw a complete, fully labeled probability tree diagram for picking two discs without replacement.
e) Calculate the probability of picking one Red and one Blue disc (in any order).
f) Imagine you pick a **third** disc without replacement. Calculate the probability that all 3 discs picked are Blue.

**Question 12: Venn Diagrams**
In a class of 40 students: 24 play Football, 16 play Basketball, and 8 play BOTH.
*Simple:*
a) How many students play Football but NOT Basketball?
b) How many students play Basketball but NOT Football?
c) How many students play NEITHER sport?
*Difficult:*
d) A student who plays Football is chosen at random. What is the probability that they also play Basketball?
e) In a different class of $50$ students, $x$ play both sports, $2x$ play ONLY Football, $3x$ play ONLY Basketball, and $2$ play neither. Set up an equation and solve for $x$.
f) Using your answer from (e), find the probability that a randomly chosen student plays EXACTLY ONE sport.

### SECTION 4: Unit 15 - Statistics & Interpreting Data

**Question 13: Scatter Graphs and Correlation**
A study compares the hours spent revising ($x$) and the test score out of 100 ($y$) for 5 students.
Data: $(1, 30), (2, 45), (3, 60), (4, 75), (5, 90)$.
*Simple:*
a) What type of correlation does this data show?
b) If a student revised for $2.5$ hours, estimate their score.
c) If a student scored $15$, roughly how many hours did they revise?
*Difficult:*
d) What is the exact equation of the line of best fit for this data (in the form $y = mx + c$)?
e) Explain the real-world meaning of the $y$-intercept in your equation.
f) Why might it be mathematically unsafe (extrapolation) to use this equation to predict the score of a student who revised for 10 hours?

**Question 14: Stem-and-Leaf and Averages**
A stem-and-leaf diagram shows the heights (in cm) of 15 plants. The stem represents tens and the leaf represents units.
$10 | 2, 5, 8$
$11 | 1, 4, 4, 7, 9$
$12 | 0, 3, 5, 6$
$13 | 1, 2, 8$
*(Key: $10 | 2 = 102 \text{ cm}$)*
*Simple:*
a) What is the height of the tallest plant?
b) What is the mode (most frequent) height?
c) Calculate the range of the heights.
*Difficult:*
d) Find the exact median height of the plants.
e) Calculate the Interquartile Range (IQR) of this data.
f) A 16th plant is added to the data. The new range becomes $40 \text{ cm}$. What are the two possible heights of this new plant?

**Question 15: Grouped Data**
The masses ($m$, in kg) of 40 parcels are recorded.
$0 < m \le 5$ (Freq: 8)
$5 < m \le 10$ (Freq: 15)
$10 < m \le 15$ (Freq: 12)
$15 < m \le 20$ (Freq: 5)
*Simple:*
a) Write down the modal class interval.
b) What is the midpoint of the class $10 < m \le 15$?
c) How many parcels weigh more than $10 \text{ kg}$?
*Difficult:*
d) Calculate an estimate for the mean mass of the parcels.
e) Explain mathematically why your answer to (d) is only an *estimate* and not an exact mean.
f) A frequency polygon is drawn for this data. What are the exact coordinates of the highest point plotted on the frequency polygon?

**Question 16: Statistical Investigations and Bias**
*Simple:*
a) State whether "The number of pages in a book" is discrete or continuous data.
b) State whether "The weight of an apple" is discrete or continuous data.
c) Define what a "biased" sample means.
*Difficult:*
d) A researcher wants to know about teenagers' eating habits. She surveys 50 people coming out of a fast-food restaurant. Give two distinct reasons why this sample is highly biased.
e) Rewrite this biased survey question to make it neutral: *"Don't you agree that school holidays should be longer?"*
f) A school has $800$ students: $400$ in Year 7, $250$ in Year 8, and $150$ in Year 9. The headteacher wants a **stratified sample** of $48$ students. Calculate exactly how many students she must choose from each Year group.
