### SECTION A: Number & Calculation (30 Marks)

**Question 1: Rational and Irrational Numbers [3 marks]**  
Look at the following list of numbers:  
$$\sqrt{9}, \quad \sqrt{8}, \quad \frac{2}{3}, \quad \pi, \quad -5, \quad 0.121212...$$  
a) Identify two **integers** from the list.  
b) Identify two **irrational numbers** from the list.

**Question 2: Surds (No Calculator) [4 marks]**    
a) Simplify $\sqrt{2} \times \sqrt{32}$.  
b) Expand and simplify: $(\sqrt{5} + 1)(\sqrt{5} - 1)$.  
c) Between which two consecutive integers does $\sqrt{70}$ lie?

**Question 3: Indices and Powers [6 marks]**  
Simplify the following, giving your answers as a single power (e.g., $3^5$):  
a) $5^4 \times 5^{-2}$  
b) $8^{-3} \div 8^{-5}$  
c) $(2^3)^4$  
d) Find the value of $x$ if $3^x = \frac{1}{27}$.

**Question 4: Standard Form Basics [4 marks]**  
a) Write $0.000452$ in standard form.  
b) Write $3.8 \times 10^5$ as an ordinary number.  
c) Explain why $45 \times 10^4$ is **not** in standard form and correct it.

**Question 5: Operations with Standard Form [5 marks]**  
Calculate the following. Give your answers in standard form:  
a) $(2.5 \times 10^4) \times (4 \times 10^3)$  
b) $(6.4 \times 10^{-3}) \div (2 \times 10^{-2})$  
c) $(3 \times 10^5) + (4.2 \times 10^6)$ *(Be careful with the powers!)*

**Question 6: Problem Solving with Numbers [8 marks]**  
a) Light travels at approximately $3 \times 10^8$ meters per second. The distance from the Sun to Mars is approximately $2.28 \times 10^{11}$ meters. Calculate how many seconds it takes for light to travel from the Sun to Mars.  
b) A cube has a volume of $10^{-6}$ $m^3$. What is the side length of the cube?

### SECTION B: Algebra & Equations (25 Marks)

**Question 7: Simplifying Expressions [4 marks]**  
Simplify fully:  
a) $4a^2 \times 3a^3$  
b) $\frac{15x^5}{3x^2}$  
c) $5(x - 2) - 2(x + 4)$

**Question 8: Solving Equations [6 marks]**  
Solve for $x$:  
a) $3x - 7 = 14$  
b) $5(x - 2) = 20$  
c) $4x + 3 = 2x - 9$

**Question 9: Equations with Fractions [4 marks]**  
Solve for $y$:  
a) $\frac{y}{4} + 2 = 5$  
b) $\frac{2y + 1}{3} = 4$

**Question 10: Inequalities [5 marks]**  
a) Solve the inequality: $3x - 5 < 10$.  
b) Represent your solution to part (a) on a number line.  
c) List the integers that satisfy: $-2 \leq n < 2$.

**Question 11: Algebraic Reasoning [6 marks]**  
The length of a rectangle is $(2x + 3)$ cm and the width is $(x - 1)$ cm.  
a) Write an expression for the **perimeter** of the rectangle. Simplify your answer.  
b) If the perimeter is $34$ cm, form an equation and solve it to find the value of $x$.  
c) Use your value of $x$ to find the **area** of the rectangle.

### SECTION C: Formulae & Changing the Subject (25 Marks)

**Question 12: Substitution [4 marks]**  
Using the formula $v = u + at$:  
a) Find $v$ when $u = 5$, $a = 3$, and $t = 4$.  
b) Find $v$ when $u = 10$, $a = -2$, and $t = 6$.  
c) Find $u$ when $v = 30$, $a = 4$, and $t = 5$.

**Question 13: Deriving Formulae [5 marks]**  
A taxi company uses the following rule for the cost of a trip:
*"A fixed charge of $3 plus $2 for every kilometer traveled."*  
a) Write a formula for the Cost ($C$) in terms of the kilometers ($k$).  
b) Use your formula to calculate the cost of a 12 km trip.  
c) If a trip costs $29, calculate the distance traveled.

**Question 14: Changing the Subject (Basic) [6 marks]**  
Make the letter in brackets the subject of the formula:  
a) $y = x + 5$  $[x]$  
b) $P = 4L$  $[L]$  
c) $y = 3x - 2$  $[x]$

**Question 15: Changing the Subject (Advanced) [6 marks]**  
Rearrange these formulae to make $x$ the subject:  
a) $y = \frac{x}{5} + 2$  
b) $T = \sqrt{x + 1}$  
c) $ax + b = c$

**Question 16: "Think Like a Mathematician" Challenge [4 marks]**  
The formula for the area of a trapezoid is:
$$A = \frac{h(a+b)}{2}$$  
a) Calculate $A$ if $h=4$, $a=3$, and $b=7$.  
b) Rearrange the formula to make $h$ the subject.
