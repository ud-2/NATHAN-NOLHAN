### SECTION A: Integers, Roots and Divisibility (15 Marks)

**Question 1: Integer Operations in Context (3 marks)**  
A submarine is operating at a depth of $-145\text{ m}$ (below sea level). 
It ascends (goes up) by $42\text{ m}$ to avoid a reef, then descends (goes down) by $88\text{ m}$ to reach the ocean floor.
*  a) Write down a mathematical expression using positive and negative integers to represent this journey. **[1 mark]**
*  b) Calculate the final depth of the submarine. **[2 marks]**

**Question 2: Deep Divisibility Logic (4 marks)**  
A five-digit number is written as **5A6B4**, where $A$ and $B$ are unknown digits (from 0 to 9).
*  a) The number is divisible by $4$. Write down all the possible values for the digit $B$. **[2 marks]**
*  b) The number is also divisible by $9$. If $B$ takes the *smallest* possible value from your answer in part (a), find the value of $A$. Show your reasoning. **[2 marks]**

**Question 3: Applying HCF and LCM (4 marks)**  
A baker has $120$ chocolate chip cookies and $84$ oatmeal cookies. 
He wants to pack them into identical gift boxes, so that each box contains the exact same number of chocolate chip cookies and the exact same number of oatmeal cookies, with none left over.
*  a) What is the greatest number of gift boxes the baker can make? **[2 marks]**
*  b) How many of each type of cookie will be in every gift box? **[2 marks]**

**Question 4: Estimating and Calculating Roots (4 marks)**  
*  a) Explain why $\sqrt{80}$ is closer to $9$ than to $8$. **[2 marks]**
*  b) Without calculating the exact value, find two consecutive integers that $\sqrt[3]{200}$ lies between. Justify your answer. **[2 marks]**

### SECTION B: Algebra - Expressions, Equations and Inequalities (18 Marks)

**Question 5: Constructing Expressions (4 marks)**  
Sarah is $y$ years old. 
* Her brother Tom is $3$ years older than Sarah.
* Her mother is exactly three times as old as Sarah.
* Her father is $5$ years older than her mother.
*  a) Write down an expression, in its simplest form, for the **total age** of all four family members. **[3 marks]**
*  b) If Sarah is $12$ years old, what is the total age of the family? **[1 mark]**

**Question 6: Geometry and Algebra (5 marks)**  
A rectangle has a length of $(3x - 2)\text{ cm}$ and a width of $(x + 5)\text{ cm}$.
*  a) Write down an expression for the **perimeter** of the rectangle. Expand and simplify your expression fully. **[2 marks]**
*  b) The perimeter of the rectangle is $54\text{ cm}$. Form an equation and solve it to find the value of $x$. **[2 marks]**
*  c) Calculate the exact length of the rectangle. **[1 mark]**

**Question 7: Formulae (5 marks)**  
A physics formula states that final velocity ($v$) is calculated using the formula: 
$$v = u + at$$
*  a) Work out the value of $v$ when $u = 12$, $a = -4$, and $t = 5$. **[2 marks]**
*  b) **Derive a new formula** (Change the subject of the formula) to make $t$ the subject. **[3 marks]**

**Question 8: Inequalities (4 marks)**  
Consider the inequality: $-3 < 2x - 1 \leq 7$
*  a) Solve the inequality to find the range of values for $x$. **[3 marks]**
*  b) List all the **integers** that satisfy this inequality. **[1 mark]**

### SECTION C: Place Value, Decimals and Rounding (17 Marks)

**Question 9: Related Calculations (4 marks)**  
You are given that: $348 \times 65 = 22620$
Use this fact to write down the answers to the following calculations (do not do the long multiplication):
*  a) $34.8 \times 6.5$ **[1 mark]**
*  b) $0.348 \times 6500$ **[1 mark]**
*  c) $22620 \div 6.5$ **[2 marks]**

**Question 10: Decimal Word Problem (5 marks)**  
A carpenter is building a bookshelf. He has a wooden board that is $3.2\text{ meters}$ long.
He cuts off $4$ shelves, each measuring $0.65\text{ meters}$ in length. 
Each cut destroys $0.02\text{ meters}$ of wood (sawdust).
*  a) What is the total length of wood used for the $4$ shelves? **[1 mark]**  
*  b) How much wood is destroyed by the $4$ cuts? **[2 marks]**  
*  c) Calculate the length of the wooden board that is left over. **[2 marks]**

**Question 11: Rounding and Estimation (8 marks)**  
*  a) Round $14.852$ to $1$ decimal place. **[1 mark]**
*  b) Round $0.0739$ to $2$ decimal places. **[1 mark]**
*  c) The population of a city is $1,458,920$. Round this number to the nearest hundred thousand. **[1 mark]**
*  d) Look at this calculation: 
$$\frac{48.7 \times 19.4}{0.52}$$
Estimate the answer to this calculation by rounding each number to the nearest whole number (or 1 significant figure) before calculating. Show your rounding stages clearly. **[3 marks]**
*  e) Why is estimation useful in mathematics? **[2 marks]**

### SECTION D: Think Like a Mathematician (10 Marks)

**Question 12: Algebraic Proof (5 marks)**  
*In mathematics, we can use algebra to prove that a pattern always works.*
Choose any three **consecutive integers** (for example: $4, 5, 6$ or $10, 11, 12$).
*  a) Add your three consecutive integers together. Divide the answer by 3. What do you notice? **[1 mark]**
*  b) Let the first integer be $n$. Write down the next two consecutive integers in terms of $n$. **[1 mark]**
*  c) Add these three algebraic terms together and simplify. **[1 mark]**
*  d) Factorise your answer to part (c) to **prove** that the sum of any three consecutive integers is always a multiple of 3. **[2 marks]**

**Question 13: The Mystery Number (5 marks)**  
Find the mystery number based on the following clues:
* Clue 1: I am a decimal number with exactly one decimal place.
* Clue 2: I am greater than $\sqrt{16}$ but less than $\sqrt{36}$.
* Clue 3: When I am multiplied by 10, the result is an odd integer.
* Clue 4: The sum of my digits is a multiple of 4.
* Clue 5: The digit in my tenths column is a prime number.

Show your logical steps to find the mystery number. **[5 marks]**
