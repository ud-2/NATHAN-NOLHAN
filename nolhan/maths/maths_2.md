### SECTION A: Number & Calculation (15 Marks)
*In this section, you must demonstrate mastery of integer operations and number properties.*

**Question 1: Complex Integer Operations [3 marks]**  
Calculate the value of the following expressions using the correct order of operations (BIDMAS):  
a) $15 - 3 \times (4 - 9)$  
b) $\frac{-20 + (-4)}{6 - (-2)}$

**Question 2: Powers and Roots Investigation [3 marks]**  
a) Calculate: $10^2 - \sqrt[3]{125} \times 2^3$  
b) Explain why $\sqrt{-16}$ is not possible to find among real integers, but $\sqrt[3]{-27}$ is possible. Give the value of $\sqrt[3]{-27}$.

**Question 3: HCF/LCM Problem Solving [3 marks]**  
Bus A arrives at the station every 12 minutes. Bus B arrives every 18 minutes. Bus C arrives every 30 minutes.
They all arrive together at 12:00 PM.
At what time will they next all arrive together? (Show your working using prime factorization).

**Question 4: Divisibility Logic [3 marks]**  
You have the four digits: **3, 5, 6, 7**.  
a) Arrange these digits to make the largest possible odd number.  
b) Arrange these digits to make a 4-digit number that is divisible by 5.  
c) Explain why it is impossible to arrange these four digits to make a number divisible by 9.

**Question 5: Prime Factors [3 marks]**  
a) Write $120$ as a product of its prime factors (use a factor tree).  
b) Use your answer to find the smallest integer $k$ such that $120 \times k$ is a square number.

### SECTION B: Algebraic Proficiency (15 Marks)
*In this section, you must simplify complex expressions and substitute values carefully.*

**Question 6: Constructing Formulae [3 marks]**  
A taxi company charges a fixed fee of \$4 dollars plus \$2 for every kilometer traveled.  
a) Write a formula for the cost $C$ dollars for a journey of $k$ kilometers.  
b) Use your formula to find the cost of a 15 km journey.  
c) If the journey costs $\$30$, how many kilometers was the trip?

**Question 7: Advanced Substitution [3 marks]**  
Given that $x = 3$, $y = -2$, and $z = 5$.
Calculate the value of these expressions:  
a) $3x - 2y$  
b) $x^2 + y^2$  
c) $\frac{x + y}{z}$

**Question 8: Simplifying with Negatives [3 marks]**  
Simplify the following expressions fully:  
a) $5a - 2b - 7a + 6b$  
b) $3xy + 4y - 2xy + x$

**Question 9: Expanding and Simplifying [3 marks]**  
Expand the brackets and then simplify:  
a) $4(x + 3) + 2(x - 5)$  
b) $5(2y - 1) - 3(y + 4)$  *(Be careful with the negative sign!)*

**Question 10: Algebraic Pyramids [3 marks]**  
In a number pyramid, the block above is the **sum** of the two blocks below it.
If the bottom row is:  $[ 2n ] \quad [ n + 3 ] \quad [ 4n - 1 ]$
Find the expression for the top block. (Show your steps).

**Question 11: Solving Equations (Variables on one side) [3 marks]**  
Solve for $x$:  
a) $4x - 7 = 13$  
b) $\frac{x}{3} + 5 = 9$

**Question 12: Equations with Brackets [3 marks]**  
Solve for $y$:  
a) $3(y + 4) = 24$

**Question 13: Setting up Equations (Geometry) [3 marks]**  
A rectangle has a width of $x$ cm and a length of $(2x + 1)$ cm.
The perimeter of the rectangle is $32$ cm.  
a) Write an equation for the perimeter.  
b) Solve the equation to find the value of $x$.

**Question 14: Inequalities on a Number Line [3 marks]**

**Question 15: Integer Ranges [3 marks]**

### SECTION D: Reasoning & "Think Like a Mathematician" (15 Marks)
*These questions require multi-step thinking and connecting Units 1 & 2.*

**Question 16: The Pattern of Squares [3 marks]**  
Look at the pattern:
$3^2 - 1^2 = 8$
$4^2 - 2^2 = 12$
$5^2 - 3^2 = 16$  
a) Write down the next line in the pattern.  
b) Use algebra to show why this pattern works. (Hint: let the numbers be $n$ and $n-2$).

**Question 17: Age Problem [4 marks]**  
Sarah is $x$ years old.
Her mother is 3 times as old as Sarah.
Her brother is 4 years younger than Sarah.
The sum of their three ages is 56.  
a) Write an expression for the mother's age and the brother's age in terms of $x$.  
b) Form an equation and solve it to find Sarah's age.

**Question 18: The HCF Logic [3 marks]**  
Two numbers, $A$ and $B$, are both less than 20.
The HCF of $A$ and $B$ is 4.
The LCM of $A$ and $B$ is 24.
Find the values of $A$ and $B$.

**Question 19: Missing Operations [2 marks]**  
Place brackets $( )$ in the following equation to make it true:
$$5 - 2 \times 3 + 4 = -5$$

**Question 20: The Magic Square [3 marks]**  
In a magic square, the sum of each row, column, and diagonal is the same.

| $x+2$ | $3$ | $4$ |
|-------|-----|-----|
| $\cdots$ | $\cdots$ | $\cdots$ |
| $6$ | $\cdots$ | $2$ |

Find the value of $x$ (Hint: Look at the diagonal with numbers).
