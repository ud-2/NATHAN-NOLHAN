### SECTION A: BIOLOGY (Units 1, 4, 7)

**Question 1: Cell Structure and Function (5 marks)**  
A student examines a cell under a light microscope. They observe a nucleus, a cell membrane, cytoplasm, and a large permanent vacuole.  
a) State whether this is a plant cell or an animal cell, and give **one** reason based *only* on the student's observations. [2]  
b) The student cannot see mitochondria using this basic light microscope. Explain why mitochondria are essential for this cell to survive. [2]  
c) Name one other structure the student might expect to find in this cell if they used a more powerful electron microscope. [1]

**Question 2: Specialised Cells (5 marks)**  
Ciliated epithelial cells line the human windpipe (trachea). They have tiny hair-like structures called cilia.  
a) Describe the main function of these ciliated cells in the respiratory system. [2]  
b) Cigarette smoke contains chemicals that paralyse the cilia. Explain why smokers often develop a severe cough. [2]  
c) State the biological term used to describe a group of similar cells working together to perform a specific function. [1]

**Question 3: Viruses and Life Processes (5 marks)**  
Scientists debate whether viruses should be classified as living organisms.  
a) List **three** of the seven life processes (characteristics of living things). [3]  
b) Explain why a virus is considered "non-living" when it is outside a host cell. [2]

**Question 4: Keys and Classification (4 marks)**
A student finds four unknown small animals (A, B, C, and D) in a pond. They use a dichotomous key to identify them.
*   Question 1: Does it have legs? 
    *   Yes $\rightarrow$ Go to Question 2
    *   No $\rightarrow$ Animal A (Leech)
*   Question 2: Does it have a shell?
    *   Yes $\rightarrow$ Animal B (Water Snail)
    *   No $\rightarrow$ Go to Question 3
*   Question 3: Does it have wings?
    *   Yes $\rightarrow$ Animal C (Diving Beetle)
    *   No $\rightarrow$ Animal D (Water Spider)

a) Using the key, state two physical characteristics of the Diving Beetle. [2]  
b) The student finds a fifth animal: a small worm with no legs and no shell. Explain why this key needs to be improved to accurately identify this new animal alongside Animal A. [2]

**Question 5: Microorganisms and Scientific Enquiry (6 marks)**  
A student investigates the growth of bacteria in milk. They place $100\text{ ml}$ of milk in three identical flasks at different temperatures ($5^\circ\text{C}$, $20^\circ\text{C}$, and $35^\circ\text{C}$) and measure the number of bacteria after 24 hours.  
a) Identify the **independent variable** in this investigation. [1]  
b) Identify the **dependent variable**. [1]  
c) State **two** control variables the student must keep the same to ensure it is a fair test. [2]  
d) Predict at which temperature the bacteria will reproduce the fastest, and explain your reasoning. [2]

**Question 6: Food Webs and Ecology (4 marks)**  
Consider a forest ecosystem where oak trees provide acorns. Squirrels and mice eat the acorns. Foxes eat both squirrels and mice. Owls eat only mice.  
a) Identify the **producer** in this ecosystem. [1]  
b) A disease greatly reduces the squirrel population. Predict and explain the immediate effect this will have on the population of mice. [3]
