## Section 1: Computational Thinking (10 points)  
*Focus: Problem solving, decomposition, and abstraction.*  

**1. (2 pts)** In Computational Thinking, what does the term **Abstraction** mean?  
**2. (2 pts)** Another key pillar of computational thinking is **Pattern Recognition**. Why is finding patterns useful when solving a complex problem?  
**3. (2 pts)** You are creating a Flowchart for an algorithm. What do the **Diamond** shape and the **Rectangle** shape represent?  
**4. (2 pts)** You need to build a system to automatically water a cocoa farm in Cameroon. **Decompose** this problem by listing two (2) specific sensors or inputs the system will need to check before turning on the water.  
**5. (2 pts)** Read the following sequence for crossing a busy road. Identify the **logical error** and explain how to fix it.
*   *Step 1:* Look left and right.
*   *Step 2:* Walk across the road.
*   *Step 3:* If a car is coming, wait.
*   *Step 4:* Reach the other side safely.

## Section 2: Algorithms & Pseudocode (10 points)  
*Focus: Logic, sequences, and programming concepts (No specific programming language).*  

**6. (2 pts)** In programming, what is a **Variable**?  
**7. (2 pts)** Explain the difference between a **Syntax Error** and a **Logic Error** in an algorithm.  
**8. (2 pts)** What does the term **Iteration** (or looping) mean in algorithm design? Give a real-life example of an iteration.  
**9. (2 pts)** Read this pseudocode:
```text
INPUT student_score
IF student_score > 50 THEN
    DISPLAY "You passed!"
ELSE
    DISPLAY "You failed."
END IF
```
What programming concept does the `IF / ELSE` structure represent? (Choose one: *Sequencing, Selection, or Iteration*).  
**10. (2 pts)** Write a short algorithm using **Pseudocode** (plain English instructions) that asks a user for their age, and if they are 18 or older, displays "Eligible to vote", otherwise displays "Too young to vote".
